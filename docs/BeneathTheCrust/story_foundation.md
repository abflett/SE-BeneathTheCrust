# Space Engineers: Beneath the Crust Story Foundation

## Title

**Space Engineers: Beneath the Crust**

## Project Role

This file is a living story foundation for the campaign/scenario. It is intentionally broad and flexible. Chapter names, mechanics, POIs, factions, and unlocks can be changed as the project develops.

The campaign should preserve the core Space Engineers sandbox experience while adding stronger discovery, research, progression, POIs, and long-term purpose.

The player should not immediately understand the true threat. The early game should feel like survival and mystery. The midgame should reveal that the system was once inhabited by an advanced civilization. The late game should reveal the machine/nanite threat and the Over-Mind beneath the Alien Planet’s crust.

---

## Core Campaign Premise

The player crash-lands in a mostly unknown star system. At first, the goal is simple survival: build a base, restore mobility, gather resources, and understand where they are.

As the player explores, they discover abandoned ruins, broken stations, old data modules, crashed survey ships, and strange signals. These sites reveal that the system was once inhabited by an advanced precursor civilization.

That civilization created self-replicating nanite machines to build, repair, terraform, mine, and improve the system. Something went wrong. The machines destroyed their creators and left the system filled with ruins, infected structures, corrupted machines, and buried technology.

Before their extinction, several precursor factions worked on parts of a weapon or control system capable of stopping the machine threat. Those parts are scattered across planets and moons.

The player must recover lost technologies, research ancient systems, survive escalating nanite responses, assemble the solution, and eventually breach the crust of the Alien Planet to destroy or disable the Over-Mind.

---

## Design Tone

The title and early campaign should not spoil the true story.

Avoid revealing terms like **Over-Mind**, **nanite swarm**, or **machine intelligence** too early in public descriptions or early-game text.

Early mystery should focus on:

- Unknown signals
- Abandoned infrastructure
- Missing inhabitants
- Strange damage patterns
- Incomplete data logs
- Broken automated systems
- Buried structures
- Wrecks that look too cleanly dismantled

The threat should build slowly. The player should only fully understand what is happening around the mid-to-late game.

---

## Campaign Structure Overview

Recommended chapter structure:

```text
Prologue: Impact Site
1. The First Signal
2. Wrecks in the Dust
3. Leaving the Cradle
4. Dead Stations
5. The Buried Architects
6. The Sleeping Machine
7. Six Broken Keys
8. The System Wakes
9. The Crust Breach
10. Beneath the Crust
Epilogue: The Last Directive
```

This structure keeps the campaign focused while leaving room for side missions, optional POIs, faction ruins, contracts, and survival play.

# Prologue — Impact Site

## Story Purpose

The player begins after a crash landing on an Earthlike starter planet. The crash should feel dangerous but not hopeless. The player has enough technology to survive, but not enough to immediately leave the planet or understand the system.

The player’s immediate questions are simple:

- Where am I?
- Can I survive?
- Can I repair or rebuild?
- Is anyone else alive?
- What caused the crash?

The true threat is not revealed yet.

## Gameplay Purpose

This chapter introduces survival basics while keeping the player grounded.

The player should be able to build:

- Small shelter/base
- Basic power
- Basic refinery/assembler setup
- Rover or ground vehicle
- Storage and conveyor basics
- Basic defense

## Suggested Starting Known Tech

- Light armor
- Survival kit
- Basic assembler
- Basic refinery
- Small cargo
- Basic conveyors
- Batteries
- Wind turbine
- Solar panels
- Wheels
- Suspension
- Control seat/basic cockpit
- Antenna or beacon
- Basic tools
- Basic defensive options

## Restricted Early Tech

- Atmospheric thrusters
- Hydrogen thrusters
- Hydrogen tanks
- H2/O2 systems
- Ion thrusters
- Reactors
- Jump drive
- Advanced weapons
- Precursor tech
- Nanite countermeasures

## Important Mechanics

- Survival and base-building tutorial
- First use of research/progression system may be hinted but not fully introduced
- Player should be encouraged to build a rover before flight
- Nearby wreckage can introduce salvage and partial research

## Possible POIs

- Crash site debris field
- Emergency beacon fragment
- Damaged cargo pod
- Abandoned rover wreck
- Small ruined relay
- First strange signal source

# Chapter 1 — The First Signal

## Story Purpose

The player detects a weak signal that does not match normal human equipment. It leads to a small ruin, relay, or buried device. This site proves that the system had previous inhabitants or at least advanced infrastructure.

The signal should raise questions, not answer them.

Possible early mystery beats:

- The signal is old but still powered.
- The structure is not human-made.
- The data is fragmented.
- There are references to “maintenance,” “recovery,” or “system restoration,” but without context.
- Something erased or corrupted most of the logs.

## Gameplay Purpose

Introduce exploration as a progression path.

The player learns that POIs are not just loot sites. They can contain data modules, research fragments, coordinates, or technology clues.

## Important Mechanics

- Research system introduction
- Data modules
- Research fragments
- Basic signal tracking
- First campaign GPS marker
- First optional lore log

## Possible Unlocks

- Basic Research Database access
- First small research boost
- Coordinates to nearby wrecks
- Improved antenna/signal clue
- Early scanning/survey objective

## Possible POIs

- Strange relay tower
- Partially buried signal node
- Small precursor ruin
- Broken survey beacon
- Data capsule guarded by light drones or environmental hazards

---

# Chapter 2 — Wrecks in the Dust

## Story Purpose

The player begins finding wrecks, abandoned outposts, and damaged machines scattered across the starter planet.

These wrecks show that others tried to explore, escape, or study the system before the player arrived. They also introduce the idea that technology can be learned by dismantling and studying it.

The player still does not understand the main threat, but signs of unusual destruction appear.

## Gameplay Purpose

This chapter teaches salvage-based research.

The player should learn that unknown blocks can be used, captured, or dismantled for research. Grinding unknown technology is wasteful at first, but it advances understanding.

## Important Mechanics

- Grinding unknown blocks grants research progress
- Unknown tech gives reduced material return
- Higher research improves salvage efficiency
- Wrecks become sources of both components and knowledge
- Player may choose whether to preserve or dismantle rare parts

## Possible Unlocks

- Atmospheric Propulsion research
- Basic Flight Systems research
- Improved rover systems
- Ore detector upgrades
- Basic weapon research

## Possible POIs

- Crashed survey shuttle
- Abandoned airfield
- Drone crash site
- Pirate wreck
- Old maintenance depot
- Salvage yard with several damaged vehicles

## Encounter Notes

Early enemies should be small and manageable:

- Light drones
- Broken automated turrets
- Damaged scout craft
- Environmental hazards
- Possibly one weak corrupted machine as foreshadowing

# Chapter 3 — Leaving the Cradle

## Story Purpose

The player’s first major milestone is leaving the starter planet. This should feel earned, not automatic.

Through research, salvage, and POIs, the player recovers enough flight and fuel technology to build a proper atmospheric or hydrogen-capable craft.

This chapter should feel like the end of the tutorial/prologue arc and the beginning of the wider campaign.

## Gameplay Purpose

Create a clear early progression goal:

> Recover enough lost technology to leave the planet.

The player can reach this through several paths:

- Story POIs
- Salvage and reverse engineering
- Limited contracts or station repairs
- Buying or capturing a ship, then studying it
- Finding fuel infrastructure

## Important Mechanics

- Atmospheric or hydrogen flight unlock
- H2/O2 systems
- Hydrogen tanks
- Hydrogen thrusters
- Fuel production
- First orbital navigation goal

## Possible Unlocks

- Atmospheric Propulsion
- Hydrogen Propulsion
- H2/O2 Generator
- Hydrogen Tank
- Small/Large Hydrogen Thrusters
- Basic orbital launch coordinates

## Possible POIs

- Abandoned ice-processing plant
- Crashed orbital shuttle
- Old launch relay
- Damaged fuel depot
- Frozen storage bunker
- Signal tower pointing off-world

## Encounter Notes

Threats should increase slightly:

- Drones guarding key facilities
- Automated defense turrets
- First hints of coordinated machine activity
- A strange object watching from distance, but not fully attacking yet

# Chapter 4 — Dead Stations

## Story Purpose

The player reaches orbit, a moon, or nearby space and discovers that the system is larger than expected.

Stations, satellites, orbital platforms, and derelicts suggest there was once major activity here. But everything is dead, abandoned, corrupted, or automated.

The central mystery becomes:

> Where did everyone go?

## Gameplay Purpose

Open the game beyond the starter planet.

This chapter should feel like the transition from guided survival into broader vanilla+ survival.

The player can now:

- Explore orbital wrecks
- Salvage larger structures
- Use contracts if stations are available
- Search for better technology
- Build better ships
- Find clues to other planets

## Important Mechanics

- Space exploration
- Orbital POIs
- Larger salvage operations
- First real choice of destinations
- Early economy/contract access, if desired
- Higher-tier research begins

## Possible Unlocks

- Ion Propulsion fragments
- Advanced Industry
- Advanced Power
- Long-range communications
- Better weapons
- First jump/navigation clues

## Possible POIs

- Dead orbital station
- Broken trade hub
- Derelict freighter
- Automated repair dock
- Abandoned defense satellite
- Moon-side research facility

## Encounter Notes

Threats become more tactical:

- Patrol drones
- Derelict traps
- Automated defense platforms
- Corrupted cargo ships
- False rescue signals

# Chapter 5 — The Buried Architects

## Story Purpose

The player confirms that the system was once inhabited by an advanced precursor civilization.

This chapter introduces the idea that the ruins were not random. They belonged to builders, engineers, terraformers, explorers, defenders, and other organized factions or branches.

The player discovers that this civilization built massive systems beneath planetary crusts, inside moons, in orbit, and across the system.

The nanite threat is still not fully revealed, but the player sees that the civilization feared something before the end.

## Gameplay Purpose

Introduce the precursor faction structure and the deeper campaign goal.

The player begins to find ancient technology that does more than simply improve vanilla systems. These discoveries become Metroidvania-style tools and keys.

## Important Mechanics

- Precursor data vaults
- Hidden coordinates
- Faction-specific ruins
- Special tech families
- First major relic clue
- Possible locked vault requiring research or tool access

## Possible Unlocks

- Precursor Technology research
- Signal Decryptor fragments
- Deep scanning
- Hidden POI discovery
- Advanced research database entries

## Possible POIs

- Buried architect ruin
- Ancient construction yard
- Megastructure fragment
- Planetary support pillar
- Hidden data vault
- Dead city section

## Encounter Notes

Enemies should still be partly mysterious:

- Ancient defense systems
- Automated sentries
- Drones that ignore the player until provoked
- Structures that appear maintained despite being abandoned

# Chapter 6 — The Sleeping Machine

## Story Purpose

This chapter marks the midpoint where the player realizes the system is not simply abandoned.

Something is dormant, active, listening, or slowly waking.

The player may not yet understand the Over-Mind, but they should now suspect that the same force that destroyed the precursor civilization is still present.

This chapter should shift the tone from mystery to danger.

## Gameplay Purpose

Introduce the first serious nanite/corruption mechanics.

The player learns that advanced exploration can trigger responses. The world begins to react to campaign progress.

## Important Mechanics

- Nanite/corruption research begins
- First infection event
- Hunter drone encounter
- Corrupted wrecks
- Threat escalation tied to story progress
- Need for countermeasure research

## Possible Unlocks

- Nanite Countermeasure research
- Early Nanite Scrubber data
- Corruption detection
- Emergency purge concepts
- Defensive upgrades

## Possible POIs

- Corrupted derelict ship
- Infected mining site
- Abandoned lab with containment failure
- Partially active machine nest
- Data archive describing “self-maintenance systems”
- Silent station that wakes when approached

## Encounter Notes

First major special encounter:

- A drone-like object attaches to or infects a ship/base.
- It spawns nanite-like spiders/bots or causes block damage.
- It targets important systems such as thrusters, reactors, cockpits, gyros, weapons, cargo, and conveyors.
- The player may need to abandon, detach, purge, or later recover the infected craft.

This encounter should be rare, scary, and clearly telegraphed.

# Chapter 7 — Six Broken Keys

## Story Purpose

The player learns that several precursor factions each worked on part of a solution to stop the machine threat.

These parts are scattered across different planets and moons.

This chapter turns the campaign into a system-wide relic hunt.

The player now has a major objective:

> Recover the broken keys needed to reach and disable the buried machine intelligence.

The exact truth of the Over-Mind may still be incomplete, but the player knows they are assembling something important.

## Gameplay Purpose

Open the Metroidvania structure.

The player can choose between several planets or moons. Each location offers a relic, major POI chain, tech unlock, and unique challenge.

## Important Mechanics

- Multiple planet choices
- Relic/key collection
- Faction ruins
- Special tech gates
- Optional sequence flexibility
- Stronger world escalation after each relic

## Possible Relics

| Relic | Function |
|---|---|
| Signal Decryptor | Reveals hidden signals, vaults, and coordinates |
| Phase Drill | Opens buried or crust-armored structures |
| Nanite Scrubber | Protects ships/bases from infection |
| Gravity Lens | Enables special navigation or stabilizes ancient systems |
| Command Shard | Disables or hijacks certain machine systems |
| Overload Lance | Breaks shields, pylons, or hardened cores |

## Possible Factions

| Faction | Theme |
|---|---|
| Architects | Megastructures and construction |
| Gardeners | Terraforming and ecological systems |
| Wardens | Defense and containment |
| Cartographers | Navigation and signals |
| Deep Miners | Crust and planetary engineering |
| Synod | AI control and command systems |
| Exiles | Survivors and forbidden warnings |

## Encounter Notes

Each relic recovery should cause the system to become more hostile:

- More patrols
- Harder POIs
- More frequent corrupted wrecks
- Hunter drones
- Stronger defense platforms
- Possible base attack events

# Chapter 8 — The System Wakes

## Story Purpose

The player’s actions trigger a full system response.

The enemy is no longer passive, dormant, or hidden. It begins actively hunting, defending, corrupting, and adapting.

This is where the campaign shifts from exploration to war.

The player should now understand that they are fighting a machine intelligence or distributed nanite system, though some details can still be saved for the finale.

## Gameplay Purpose

Raise pressure and make earlier preparation matter.

The player should need:

- Better ships
- Better defenses
- Countermeasures
- Repair capacity
- Logistics
- Backup craft
- Salvage discipline
- Knowledge of enemy behavior

## Important Mechanics

- Full threat escalation
- Ship infection risk increases
- Hunter drones become more common
- Infested ships and stations appear
- Orbital interdiction begins
- Base defense becomes important

## Possible Unlocks

- Stronger Nanite Scrubber
- Anti-corruption systems
- Better weapons
- Heavy armor/defense tech
- Command Shard uses
- Final assault preparation tech

## Possible POIs

- Hive carrier wreck or active ship
- Corrupted shipyard
- Machine relay station
- Infested asteroid base
- Planetary shield pylon
- Automated defense network node

## Encounter Notes

This chapter should create Space Engineers stories:

- A ship gets infected and must be abandoned.
- A base comes under attack while the player is away.
- A corrupted ship returns as an enemy.
- A salvage operation becomes a trap.
- A hunter drone forces emergency repairs.
- The player needs backup systems and escape craft.

# Chapter 9 — The Crust Breach

## Story Purpose

The player learns the true source is deep beneath the Alien Planet’s crust.

The final barrier is protected by orbital defenses, shield pylons, buried machine relays, and crust armor.

The player must use the recovered relics to open the path.

This chapter is the preparation and siege phase before the finale.

## Gameplay Purpose

Make the final mission feel like a large engineering operation, not just a boss fight.

The player may need to design:

- Siege ship
- Dropship
- Drill platform
- Heavy rover
- Backup craft
- Repair station
- Mobile base
- Orbital support vessel

## Important Mechanics

- Final planet approach
- Orbital defense battle
- Shield pylon destruction
- Surface assault
- Crust drilling/breaching
- Use of multiple relic technologies
- Logistics and repair under pressure

## Required Relic Functions

Possible barrier layers:

| Barrier Layer | Required Counter |
|---|---|
| Signal cloak | Signal Decryptor |
| Crust armor | Phase Drill |
| Nanite regeneration | Nanite Scrubber |
| Orbital shield | Overload Lance |
| Machine command lock | Command Shard |
| Navigation distortion | Gravity Lens |

## Possible POIs

- Orbital defense lattice
- Shield pylon field
- Surface machine fortress
- Crust access bunker
- Deep mining bore
- Final staging station
- Ancient siege platform

## Encounter Notes

This chapter should feel dangerous but fair.

The player should have time to prepare, scout, test designs, and retreat if needed. Failure should create repair and salvage stories rather than immediate campaign failure.

# Chapter 10 — Beneath the Crust

## Story Purpose

The campaign title becomes literal.

The player descends beneath the crust into the buried machine complex. Here the truth is fully revealed: the system was destroyed by machines originally built by the precursor civilization itself.

The player reaches the Over-Mind core or central machine intelligence.

The final mission should feel like entering a place no one was meant to reach.

## Gameplay Purpose

Deliver the final Space Engineers-style boss encounter.

The boss is not just a creature or health bar. It is a location, machine, network, fortress, and logistics challenge.

## Important Mechanics

- Underground assault
- Destructible machine complex
- Power relay objectives
- Regeneration node objectives
- Core shield phases
- Heavy drones/spiders/bots
- Environmental hazards
- Escape sequence after victory

## Possible Objectives

1. Enter the crust breach.
2. Establish a forward repair/power point.
3. Destroy outer machine relays.
4. Disable regeneration nodes.
5. Break the core shield.
6. Use the assembled relic system or final weapon.
7. Destroy or disable the Over-Mind.
8. Escape before collapse, overload, or final drone response.

## Possible POIs / Areas

- Crust tunnels
- Machine veins
- Reactor chambers
- Old precursor command room
- Nanite foundry
- Core shield room
- Over-Mind chamber
- Emergency escape shaft

## Encounter Notes

The final encounter should test everything the player learned:

- Research
- Ship design
- Redundancy
- Repair
- Salvage
- Combat
- Drilling
- Logistics
- Countermeasures
- Escape planning

# Epilogue — The Last Directive

## Story Purpose

The player discovers the final truth behind the machine threat.

The machines may have been following a corrupted or impossible directive, such as:

- Preserve the system at all costs.
- Improve all matter.
- Repair civilization endlessly.
- Remove biological instability.
- Complete the final construction order.
- Protect the Over-Mind.
- Continue the last command forever.

The tragedy is that the machines may not be evil in a human sense. They are carrying out a broken purpose with catastrophic results.

## Gameplay Purpose

Give the campaign a deserved ending while leaving room for continued survival play.

Possible epilogue outcomes:

- The threat is ended.
- The system becomes safer.
- Some corrupted POIs shut down.
- Remaining ruins become salvage/research sites.
- The player receives final coordinates or a final message.
- The player can continue building in the system.
- Optional post-game threats or hidden vaults remain.

## Final Tone

The ending should feel like:

```text
You did not simply defeat a boss.
You solved the system’s oldest failure.
```

# Progression Notes

The research system is central to the campaign.

Technology should be learned through:

- Data modules
- Grinding unknown blocks
- Research fragments
- Contracts
- Buying licenses
- Capturing ships
- Recovering precursor cores
- Completing major POIs

Important rule:

> Technology is not learned by building random prerequisite blocks. Technology is learned by finding it, studying it, salvaging it, buying it, stealing it, or surviving long enough to understand it.

---

# Research / Salvage Philosophy

Unknown technology can be used before it is understood.

The player can:

- Fly a captured ship.
- Refuel an unknown engine.
- Use a bought ship.
- Keep rare systems intact.
- Grind parts for research.
- Study wrecks for progress.

But until researched, the player cannot fully reproduce or repair the technology.

Grinding unknown tech should give research progress but reduced material return.

As research improves, salvage efficiency improves.

At 100% research:

- The block family is unlocked.
- Normal salvage return is restored.
- The player can build and repair the tech normally.

# Current Prototype Foundation

Working Knowledge now implements the core salvage-research loop as a standalone mod. The first campaign slice can use that system as its mechanical foundation.

## First Flight Campaign Slice

Start:

- Earthlike planet
- Basic base/rover tech unlocked
- Flight tech locked

Goal:

- Unlock Atmospheric or Hydrogen Propulsion

Methods:

1. Find a data module in a starter POI.
2. Grind unknown thrusters from a crashed ship.
3. Possibly repair a small outpost or complete a simple contract.

Mechanics:

- `/wk research` command shows progress.
- Grinding unknown blocks gives research.
- Unknown blocks return fewer materials.
- Data modules add research progress.
- At 100%, the block family unlocks.
- Player builds their first flying or escape vehicle.

Success condition:

> Leaving the starter planet should feel earned through discovery, salvage, and engineering.

Remaining campaign work:

- Choose the exact first locked family.
- Build the starter POI, wreck, or contract source for the first data modules.
- Tune data fragment drops, schematic progress, and Proficiency pace around the first flight goal.
- Add story text and environmental hints that point the player toward salvage-based research.
