# Space Engineers: Beneath the Crust

## Project Identity

**Space Engineers: Beneath the Crust** is a vanilla+ campaign/scenario project that keeps the core sandbox survival experience, but adds a stronger layer of discovery, research, story progression, POIs, enemy threats, and endgame purpose.

The goal is not to turn Space Engineers into a strict RPG. The goal is to make survival feel more meaningful by adding:

- Discovery-based progression
- Ancient lore and mystery
- Metroidvania-style technology gates
- POI-driven exploration
- Research and reverse engineering
- Escalating nanite threat
- A grand campaign arc with a real ending

Short version:

> **Space Engineers, but with Empyrion-style adventure, discovery, POIs, tech recovery, and a real endgame threat.**

---

## Core Design Philosophy

Vanilla Space Engineers progression can feel dry because it is mostly a flat unlock tree:

```text
Build a basic block
→ unlock the next block branch
→ repeat
```

This project replaces that feeling with engineering discovery:

```text
Find wreckage
→ recover data
→ grind unknown blocks
→ research technology
→ unlock blueprints
→ build better machines
→ reach harder locations
```

The player learns technology through:

- Exploration
- Salvage
- Reverse engineering
- POIs
- Contracts
- Buying ships or licenses
- Capturing enemy technology
- Recovering ancient data modules

The player should still feel like they are playing Space Engineers: mining, building, grinding, welding, crashing, repairing, salvaging, and designing ships.

## Story / Lore Premise

The player crash-lands in a mostly unknown star system.

This system was once inhabited by an advanced precursor civilization. They were far beyond normal human engineering and may be the origin of mysterious high-end technology, including Prototech-like systems.

This ancient race created self-replicating nanite machines to improve the system. The machines were originally designed to:

- Build
- Repair
- Mine
- Terraform
- Maintain infrastructure
- Construct megastructures
- Improve the system automatically

But the nanites went haywire.

They destroyed or consumed their creators, leaving behind:

- Ruins
- Abandoned labs
- Ancient stations
- Buried structures
- Dead cities
- Broken megaprojects
- Old ships
- Data vaults
- Precursor tech fragments
- Nanite-infested zones

Before the civilization collapsed, several factions or branches of the precursor race worked on pieces of a weapon or system that could stop the nanite Over-Mind.

Those weapon parts were scattered across different planets and moons.

The player must recover these pieces, understand what happened, rebuild the ancient countermeasure, and eventually assault the Over-Mind deep beneath the crust of the Alien Planet.

## Main Campaign Arc

The campaign begins as basic survival and grows into a system-wide war against the nanite machine.

Possible arc:

1. Crash-land on an Earthlike planet.
2. Build a basic base and rover.
3. Discover strange ruins and data logs.
4. Learn that the system was once inhabited.
5. Recover basic flight technology.
6. Leave the starter planet.
7. Explore moons and other planets.
8. Discover precursor factions and their lost weapon parts.
9. Unlock special tools and countermeasures.
10. Trigger stronger nanite responses.
11. Disable the Over-Mind’s protection layers.
12. Assault the Alien Planet.
13. Breach the crust.
14. Destroy the Over-Mind.
15. Reach a deserved campaign ending.

The final victory should feel earned because the player:

- Built the ships
- Learned the technology
- Visited major worlds
- Survived escalating threats
- Recovered ancient relics
- Assembled the anti-nanite solution

## Precursor Factions / Weapon Parts

The ancient race should not feel like one simple group. Different factions, cities, or branches each worked on part of the anti-nanite weapon.

Each faction could have:

- A unique planet or moon
- Distinct ruins or architecture
- Different POI style
- Different technology theme
- Different puzzle or gate
- Different lore perspective
- One major relic or weapon component

| Faction | Theme | Possible Relic |
|---|---|---|
| Architects | Megastructures, gravity, construction | Gravity Lens |
| Gardeners | Terraforming, biology, ecology | Nanite Scrubber |
| Wardens | Defense, military, containment | Overload Lance |
| Cartographers | Signals, navigation, astronomy | Signal Decryptor |
| Deep Miners | Crust, drilling, planetary engineering | Phase Drill |
| Synod | AI control, machine communication | Command Shard |
| Exiles | Survivors who knew the truth | Final weapon key |

Each relic should be both a story object and a gameplay unlock.

## Metroidvania-Style Gates

The campaign should use gates, but not simple “blue key opens blue door” logic.

The gates should feel like Space Engineers engineering problems.

Examples:

- Need hydrogen tech to leave the starter planet.
- Need signal tech to find hidden POIs.
- Need phase/drill tech to enter buried ruins.
- Need nanite scrubber tech to survive infestations.
- Need overload weapon tech to destroy shield pylons.
- Need command tech to disable certain machines.
- Need multiple relics to drop the Over-Mind barrier.

The player can often choose which direction to go first, but certain areas, planets, vaults, or threats become practical only after recovering the right technology.

## Starter Planet Concept

The first planet acts like a tutorial/prologue, but still feels like survival.

The player crash-lands on an Earthlike planet after some kind of infestation or disaster. They are a lone survivor in a mostly empty or poorly explored system.

At the start, they have enough technology to survive and build basic ground infrastructure.

### Starting Known Tech

- Light armor
- Basic refinery
- Basic assembler
- Survival kit
- Basic cargo
- Conveyors
- Batteries
- Wind power
- Solar power
- Wheels
- Suspension
- Basic cockpit or control seat
- Basic antenna or beacon
- Basic weapons
- Rover construction

### Early Locked or Restricted Tech

- Atmospheric thrusters
- Hydrogen thrusters
- Hydrogen tanks
- H2/O2 systems
- Ion thrusters
- Reactors
- Jump drive
- Advanced weapons
- Prototech
- Nanite countermeasures

### First Major Goal

> Recover enough lost technology to build a ship and leave the planet.

### Possible Starter Planet POIs

- Crashed survey shuttle
- Abandoned ice-processing station
- Old launch relay
- Infested mining site
- Damaged trade outpost
- Pirate wreck
- Precursor beacon ruin

These POIs teach or reveal the first stages of flight, hydrogen, fuel, and orbital navigation.

## Vanilla Freedom vs Campaign Gating

The preferred design is a hybrid.

The project should not completely remove vanilla Space Engineers freedom. Mining, building, salvage, contracts, trading, captured ships, and open survival should remain valid.

But progression should feel more meaningful.

The preferred structure:

```text
Keep vanilla survival mostly intact.
Revamp how block knowledge is unlocked.
Use POIs and research as the fastest progression path.
Allow other paths like contracts, salvage, buying ships, and reverse engineering.
```

The player can buy or capture advanced technology before understanding it, but there is a catch:

```text
They can use it.
They can fly it.
They can refuel it.
They can maybe repair armor around it.

But they cannot build new copies or fully repair destroyed advanced parts
until they research the technology.
```

This creates sandbox choices:

- Do I use this rare ship carefully?
- Do I grind it down to study it?
- Do I keep it intact until I understand it better?
- Do I buy tech through contracts?
- Do I raid POIs for data modules?
- Do I capture enemy technology?

## New Research / Progression System

Instead of vanilla progression, technology is unlocked through research progress.

Each tech family has a percentage or research point value.

Example:

```text
Atmospheric Propulsion: 45%
Hydrogen Propulsion: 0%
Ion Propulsion: 0%
Advanced Power: 20%
Jump Navigation: 0%
Nanite Countermeasures: 0%
Precursor Technology: 5%
```

Research can come from:

- Data modules found in POIs
- Data fragments from cargo containers
- Grinding unknown blocks
- Studying wrecks
- Buying licenses from factions or stations
- Completing contracts
- Capturing and dismantling enemy ships
- Recovering special precursor cores

This supports multiple playstyles.

| Playstyle | Progression Method |
|---|---|
| Explorer | Finds data modules and ruins |
| Salvager | Grinds wrecks and studies parts |
| Trader | Does contracts and buys licenses |
| Raider | Captures enemy ships and steals tech |
| Builder | Uses researched tech to reach harder areas |

---

## Research by Grinding Unknown Blocks

When the player grinds down a block they have not researched, they gain research progress.

But because they do not understand the technology yet, they recover fewer materials.

Example:

```text
Player finds hydrogen thrusters before researching hydrogen tech.

They grind one down.

They only recover limited components,
but gain Hydrogen Propulsion research progress.
```

As research increases, salvage efficiency improves.

### Possible Material Recovery Curve

| Research Progress | Material Recovery |
|---:|---:|
| 0–19% | Very poor return |
| 20–39% | Low return |
| 40–59% | Medium return |
| 60–79% | Good return |
| 80–99% | Almost full return |
| 100% | Normal full salvage |

Unknown tech becomes valuable in two ways:

```text
Keep it intact and use it
or
sacrifice it for research
```

This prevents players from buying or capturing one ship and instantly gaining full value from dismantling it.

A ship might only have:

- 1 cockpit
- 2 gyros
- 4 hydrogen thrusters
- 1 reactor
- 1 jump drive

Grinding that ship helps, but may not fully unlock everything. The player still needs POIs, contracts, or more salvage.

---

## Data Modules and Research Fragments

Data modules are the cleaner, faster discovery path.

Instead of instantly unlocking a block, they add research progress.

Examples:

| Item | Effect |
|---|---|
| Hydrogen Data Fragment | +15% Hydrogen Propulsion |
| Damaged Flight Computer | +20% Basic Flight Systems |
| Precursor Data Core | +40% Precursor Technology |
| Encrypted Nanite Sample | +10% Nanite Countermeasures |
| Jump Navigation Archive | +25% Jump Navigation |

This lets POIs feel rewarding without making progression too simple.

A major story POI can give a large boost, while smaller POIs give fragments.

Progression becomes:

```text
Find a data fragment
→ grind some wreckage
→ complete a contract
→ reach 100%
→ unlock the tech family
```

## Tech Families

Research should probably be grouped by technology family, not individual block, so the system does not become tedious.

Possible tech families:

- Ground Engineering
- Atmospheric Propulsion
- Hydrogen Propulsion
- Ion Propulsion
- Basic Flight Systems
- Advanced Power
- Industrial Systems
- Weapon Systems
- Jump Navigation
- Nanite Countermeasures
- Precursor Technology
- Prototech Systems
- Over-Mind Weapon Systems

Example:

```text
Hydrogen Propulsion unlocks:
- H2/O2 Generator
- Hydrogen Tank
- Small Hydrogen Thruster
- Large Hydrogen Thruster
```

This is cleaner than researching every block separately.

## Economy and Contracts

Contracts should remain relevant because they offer an alternate path.

The economy could allow players to:

- Earn money
- Buy ships
- Buy components
- Buy limited licenses
- Access faction technology
- Complete contracts for research rewards

However, on the starter planet, normal stations might be removed, damaged, or limited so the player cannot instantly skip the first survival arc.

Possible economy setup:

```text
No full stations on starter planet
or
One damaged trade outpost with limited goods
or
Advanced station services locked behind reputation/story progress
```

Later, once the player leaves the starter planet, the game opens into a more normal vanilla+ survival experience with contracts and trading.

## Ship Infection Encounter

One major encounter idea is a drone-like object that can attach to or infect the player’s ship.

Once infected, it begins spawning nanite-like spiders or bots that attack important systems.

The infection targets:

- Reactors
- Batteries
- Thrusters
- Cockpits
- Gyros
- Oxygen systems
- Weapons
- Cargo
- Conveyors
- Control blocks

The player may need to:

- Purge the infection
- Detach the infected ship section
- Abandon ship
- Escape in a smaller craft
- Recover the wreck later
- Destroy their own corrupted ship
- Retrieve important cargo from the infected vessel

This should be rare, scary, and telegraphed.

It should create Space Engineers stories, not just delete the player’s ship unfairly.

Example outcome:

```text
Your advanced ship gets infected.
You are forced to abandon it.
Later, a side mission appears to recover your cargo,
destroy the corrupted core,
or reclaim the wreck.
```

## Final Over-Mind Arc

The Over-Mind is protected deep under the Alien Planet’s crust.

It has several layers of defense, each tied to one of the relics or weapon parts.

Possible protection layers:

- Signal cloak
- Orbital defense lattice
- Nanite regeneration field
- Crust armor
- AI command lock
- Core shield

Each recovered relic disables or bypasses one layer.

The final sequence could involve:

1. Destroying orbital defenses.
2. Landing under heavy attack.
3. Disabling shield pylons.
4. Drilling into the crust.
5. Fighting through underground ruins.
6. Destroying reactor or cooling nodes.
7. Using the completed weapon.
8. Escaping before collapse or detonation.

The final boss should be a Space Engineers-style challenge:

```text
Not just a health bar.
A place.
A machine.
A siege.
A logistics challenge.
A ship-building test.
```

## UI Direction

Initial UI can be simple.

### MVP

Use chat commands:

```text
/wk help
/wk research
/wk res
/wk prof
```

Example output:

```text
Atmospheric Propulsion: 100%
Hydrogen Propulsion: 42%
Ion Propulsion: 0%
Advanced Power: 18%
Jump Navigation: 0%
Nanite Countermeasures: 0%
```

### Current And Later UI

Working Knowledge now provides the first in-game UI layer through `/wk` commands, Research Pedestal terminal controls, the Research Sci-Fi Terminal block, and selectable LCD apps for Research, Proficiency, Identity, and display calibration.

Possible future options:

- Richer Research Terminal schematic trade and sync workflows
- Custom HUD using a UI framework
- Modified or replaced progression screen
- Full research database tab

A true inventory/GPS-style tab would be more involved and should come later.

## Current Standalone Mod Layer

Working Knowledge now covers the original first research-loop target as a standalone mod:

- Grinding unknown blocks grants schematic research and hands-on Proficiency.
- Completed schematic families unlock through vanilla progression.
- Player/faction research and player-only Proficiency survive save/load.
- Salvage recovery, weld botches, scrap return, and build placement enforcement are implemented.
- `/wk` commands, Research Pedestal terminal controls, and LCD apps expose current progress in game.
- Hand tools and ship tools are supported through the current attribution paths.

The campaign slice should now build content around that working layer rather than re-prototyping the mechanics.

Useful next campaign tests:

1. Pick the first locked family, likely Atmospheric Propulsion or Hydrogen Propulsion.
2. Build the starter POI or wreck that teaches salvage-based research.
3. Tune data fragment drops and grind progress so first flight feels earned.
4. Confirm the first escape vehicle path works with progression disabled in the world settings and Working Knowledge enabled.

If that feels good, expand to Hydrogen escape, orbital POIs, ion tech, reactors, jump navigation, nanite countermeasures, precursor relics, and the final campaign arc.

---

## Design Rule

The project should follow this rule:

> **Technology is not learned by building random prerequisite blocks. Technology is learned by finding it, studying it, salvaging it, buying it, stealing it, or surviving long enough to understand it.**

This keeps the campaign powerful without destroying sandbox freedom.

The player can:

- Ignore the story and play survival.
- Do contracts and buy their way upward.
- Raid POIs and progress quickly.
- Salvage wrecks and reverse-engineer tech.
- Steal ships before understanding them.
- Use advanced parts before they can build them.
- Eventually master everything through research.

## Final Vision

The final project is a Space Engineers campaign/survival overhaul where:

```text
You build because you need to reach ruins.
You explore because technology is hidden in the world.
You salvage because wrecks contain knowledge.
You research because unknown tech is wasteful to dismantle.
You fight because nanites and corrupted machines guard the system.
You travel because each planet holds part of the solution.
You finish because the Over-Mind must be destroyed.
```

It should feel like vanilla Space Engineers gained:

- Purpose
- Discovery
- Escalation
- POIs
- Ancient mystery
- Better progression
- Real endgame direction

The campaign does not replace the sandbox.

It gives the sandbox a reason to matter.
