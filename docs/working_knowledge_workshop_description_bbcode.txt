[h1]Working Knowledge[/h1]
[h3]A salvage-first progression overhaul for Space Engineers survival.[/h3]

Working Knowledge turns technology unlocks into a salvage, research, and hands-on practice loop.

Instead of racing through a simple build-one-block chain, your engineer learns by recovering schematic data, decoding research fragments, and practicing the work. Wrecks, captured ships, abandoned stations, signals, and POIs become opportunities to study unfamiliar machines and bring that knowledge home.

[b]Research[/b] is what your engineer understands.
[b]Proficiency[/b] is how well your engineer can do the work.

Do you preserve a useful part, move it, repair around it, strip it for materials, or grind it down for research progress? Working Knowledge makes that choice matter.

[h2]0.10.0 Update: Cleaner Progress Feedback[/h2]
Working Knowledge now includes a compact Text HUD API-backed progress overlay for research and Proficiency updates. Recent schematic progress appears as small on-screen bar rows instead of routine chat spam.

By default, players see up to five recent rows in the top-right corner. Each row shows the schematic name, research progress, and Proficiency progress. Routine chat progress and popup progress toasts are off by default, but can be enabled per player.

[code]/wk config
/wk config help
/wk config progressHudEnabled true
/wk config progressHudRows 5
/wk config progressHudPosition topRight
/wk config progressHudFadeSeconds 6
/wk config progressChatEnabled true
/wk config progressToastEnabled true[/code]

The overlay can be moved to any corner or the center, offset from that preset, reordered, resized from one to ten rows, or kept visible with [b]progressHudFadeSeconds 0[/b].

[h2]How It Plays[/h2]
Unknown schematic families are locked until researched. You can recover progress by dismantling unfamiliar blocks, decoding Data Fragments, or receiving exact Data Schematics as authored rewards.

Learning the schematic unlocks the block family. Proficiency then tracks the practical side of engineering. Early work with unfamiliar systems can be inefficient: salvage may be less clean, construction may be less reliable, and repairs may cost more than expected. As your engineer gains experience, the work becomes more dependable.

[i]Technology is not just unlocked. It is learned.[/i]

[h2]Configuration[/h2]
World admins can tune difficulty, research gain, Proficiency gain, salvage recovery, construction setbacks, botch damage, and feedback.

Players can tune their own feedback separately, including HUD bars, chat progress, popup progress toasts, completion sounds, and botch sounds.

Start with:
[code]/wk
/wk help
/wk difficulty
/wk config
/wk config world[/code]

[h2]Compatibility Notes[/h2]
Working Knowledge changes block knowledge, schematic unlocks, grinding research, salvage recovery, and low-Proficiency construction. Mods that also change those systems should be tested together.

Progression overhauls are the most likely to overlap. Mods that bypass block placement restrictions, rewrite grinding or welding, or heavily change salvage flow may also affect the intended experience.

[h2]Current Status And Feedback[/h2]
Working Knowledge is a public feedback release. The core systems are playable, but balance, multiplayer edge cases, dedicated-server behavior, and compatibility with larger mod stacks are still being tested.
